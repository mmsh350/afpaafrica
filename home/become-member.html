BECOME A MEMBER

It is of course a worthy decision to become a member of the Accredited Financial Planners Association. Membership into the Association follows is permitted to all interested and qualified persons through two designated pathways - College Graduate Pathway and the Career Planner Pathway.


The College Graduate Pathway accepts applicants that had previously studied management or mathematical sciences (including information technology) at a bachelor’s degree level or equivalent (such as Nigeria’s Higher National Diploma and NSQ Level 5 in related field). Accepted applicants will then be required to take FP Level 1 curriculum and examinations in four subject areas of financial planning. After successful completion, candidates would be designated Registered Financial Planner (RFP) and be recognised as having intermediate financial planning skills equivalent to a bachelor’s degree in the subject area based on the Nigerian Skills Qualification Framework level 5.


In order to proceed to the expert level to become an Accredited Financial Planner (AFP), a certified RFP candidate must then engage in the practice of financial planning in the FP firms or in employment for a period of two calendar years and thereafter qualifies to apply. The candidate will then be required to attend a 12 weeks Financial Planning Proficiency (FPP) course (Level 2) and write a test at the end of the programme. Successful candidates will then be registered for induction as Accredited Financial Planners (AFP).


The second pathway - the Career Financial Planner Pathways - is a limited time opportunity for professionals who have all requirements for College Graduate entry plus a masters degree or recognised relevant professional qualification with an evidence of practical experience in financial planning practice. The candidate will be exempted from taking Level 1 course and exams, and proceed to level 2.
